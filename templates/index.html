About

Anemia: low red blood cells or hemoglobin causing oxygen shortage.

Symptoms:fatigue,weakness,shortness of breath,pale skin.

Causes: nutritinal deficiencies ,diseases blood loss.Treatment vary

Hemoglobin

Hemoglobin, a protein in red blood cells,transports oxygen and carbon dioxide. Healthy ranges:Men:13.2-16.6 g/dl. Women:11.6-15 g/dl

MCH

Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin (MCH) measures the average amount of hemoglobin in your red blood cells.

MCHC

Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin (MCH) measures the average quantity of hemoglobin in single red blood cells.Normal Range is between 27.5 and 33.2 picogram.

MCV

Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin Concentration (MCHC) is average concentration of hemoglobin in red blood cells.The normal range: 33.4- 35.5 g/dl.
